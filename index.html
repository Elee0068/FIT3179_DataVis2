Global Wine Report

From Droughts to Deluge


In 2022, global wine production reached 258 million hectoliters (mhl), with a slight 1% decrease from the previous year. Europe managed to maintain production levels despite challenges like heatwaves and droughts, led by Italy, France, and Spain, which saw a notable 21% increase. Germany also grew, while other EU nations faced declines.

Outside the EU, Russia and Georgia increased production, but Moldova and Switzerland saw declines. In Asia, China's wine production dropped by 29%. North America faced challenges, resulting in a 7% decrease in US production. Overall, 2022 showcased the wine industry's resilience in the face of diverse climatic challenges and success stories across the globe.

Global Wine Consumption in Flux

In 2022, global wine consumption witnessed a 1% decrease, totaling 223 million hectoliters (mhl), continuing a declining trend that began in 2018. The primary driver of this trend was the consistent reduction in wine consumption in China, which had been decreasing by 2 mhl annually since 2018, with the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 further amplifying the decline.

Global Wine Trade in Turmoil

In the realm of international wine trade, 2022 bore the brunt of formidable challenges. After the disruptions caused by the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, a year of record-high exports seemed to signal recovery.

However, the wine trade was severely impacted by the war in Ukraine and a global energy crisis that spurred inflation across major economies. Furthermore, 2022 was marked by significant supply chain disruptions, causing a slowdown in sea freight.

These combined events led to a reduction in volume of wine exports, but the silver lining was a substantial increase in average prices, soaring by 9% compared to 2021. Consequently, the global wine export value reached an all-time high of 37.6 billion EUR.
