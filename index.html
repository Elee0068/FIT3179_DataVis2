Global Wine Report

From Droughts to Deluge

In 2022, world wine production reached 258 million hectoliters (mhl), showing a slight decrease of 1% from 2021. This decrease was due to various weather challenges in different regions, with Europe and the USA overcoming heatwaves and droughts to maintain production levels. Italy, France, and Spain led the global wine production, with France seeing a remarkable 21% increase. Germany also experienced growth, while other EU countries faced declines.

Outside the EU, Russia and Georgia increased production, while Moldova and Switzerland experienced declines. In Asia, China's wine production continued its decade-long decline, dropping 29%. In North America, the USA faced frost, drought, and reduced water supply, resulting in a 7% decrease in production

In the Southern Hemisphere, South American countries saw production drops due to variable weather, with Chile leading despite a 7% decrease. Argentina and Brazil had production declines, while South Africa's production stabilized after years of drought. In Oceania, Australia experienced a 14% reduction in production, while New Zealand had a record-high harvest due to favorable conditions and high demand.

Overall, the wine industry in 2022 was marked by a dynamic interplay of climatic challenges and success stories across the globe, showcasing the resilience of the wine-producing countries.


Global Wine Consumption in Flux

In 2022, global wine consumption witnessed a 1% decrease, totaling 232 million hectoliters (mhl), continuing a declining trend that began in 2018. The primary driver of this trend was the consistent reduction in wine consumption in China, which had been decreasing by 2 mhl annually since 2018, with the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 further amplifying the decline.

The 2022 landscape was shaped by various factors, including lockdown measures, disruptions in the hospitality and tourism sectors, and increased production and distribution costs, leading to higher wine prices for consumers. The European Union (EU) remained a significant consumer, accounting for 48% of global consumption, but even there, consumption decreased by 2%.

The USA saw its wine consumption nearly return to pre-pandemic levels, making it the largest consumer globally. In contrast, China's wine consumption fell significantly by 16%, impacting global consumption trends. The landscape varied across regions, reflecting a complex interplay of economic, geopolitical, and consumer preference factors.






Global Wine Trade in Turmoil

In the realm of international wine trade, 2022 bore the brunt of formidable challenges. After the disruptions caused by the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, a year of record-high exports seemed to signal recovery.

However, the wine trade was severely impacted by the war in Ukraine and a global energy crisis that spurred inflation across major economies. Furthermore, 2022 was marked by significant supply chain disruptions, causing a slowdown in sea freight.

These combined events led to a reduction in the volume of wine exports, but the silver lining was a substantial increase in average prices, soaring by 9% compared to 2021. Consequently, the global wine export value reached an all-time high of 37.6 billion EUR. Notably, France remained the world's leading wine exporter in 2022, with exports worth 12.3 billion EUR, reflecting a reshaped landscape where price hikes were primarily driven by higher costs faced by industry stakeholders.
